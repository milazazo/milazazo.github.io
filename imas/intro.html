MILAZA

3D 2D PROJECT STORE COMMISSION MISC.
THE iDOLM@STER is a massive multimedia franchise with over 300 characters and many more songs that started in 2005. The first games for the series were Japan-exclusive arcade games, and it has since expanded into mobile games, anime, manga, concerts, and so on.

The story of the iM@S games generally follow a group of idols trying to become the "top idols" with the support of you, their Producer (more like an agent/manager). There are five "main" iDOLM@STER branches each with their own appeal. But even splitting the franchise down, it's still so huge: how do you get "into" it? In my opinion, it differs for each series. This is my iM@S intro guide for English-only speakers, from the perspective of a five-year long fan.

This guide is a work in progress.

THE iDOLM@STER (765AS)
THE iDOLM@STER (765AS) is the original series that started it all! It's also the least overwhelming in my opinion as it only has 13 main characters. A lot of 765AS content is untranslated due to them being featured in the full length console games, but luckily, they appear in Million Live! (see below) as well.
  1. Anime: The iDOLM@STER 2011 anime is easily the best out of all of the iM@S anime*. It covers all of the girls, and focuses on the main trio of Haruka, Chihaya, and Miki in particular. There's an actual plot and a decent amount of music from the series is shown. As well, it's possible to watch it on Crunchyroll, making it accesible to English viewers.
  2. Songs: Songs will be ranked highly for all of the branches since it's a music franchise afterall, but due to 765AS being the oldest and having the fewest characters, it has the best songs-per-character ratio.
  3. Million Live!: More info below.
  4. iM@S One For All!: I haven't actually played this yet, but it's the only console iM@S game that's been fully translated into English, so it's probably a good idea to check it out if you're looking for a longer deep dive into the characters. The patch is available here.
  5. Manga: There's a couple of fan translated manga, but not all of them follow the accepted 'canon' of the series. Still, they're quite enjoyable and I'd reccomend searching them out.
* There is actually another, earlier iM@S anime called Xenoglossia, but it's like, a mecha anime that has no relation to the actual iM@S plot or characters so don't worry about it.
Cinderella Girls
Cinderella Girls is the first mobile spinoff. It has an absolutely insane cast of over 200 characters, including R*amu. CG has two different games: Cinderella Girls (Mobamas), which is playable in-browser, and Starlight Stage (Deresute), a rhythm game with 3D models.
  1. Deresute: Deresute is super fun! There's a variety of different difficulty settings and a ton of songs, plus 3D models for every idol; you can even watch gorgeous music videos with your own custom team setup. This is the game I reccomend for getting into iM@S; yes, the amount of characters is absolutely overwhelming, but you'll pretty quickly find a girl you like. You can download the game here.
  2. Songs: CG has a ton of music, although it's the only iM@S series that doesn't have every character fully voiced. As well, some characters only have a few songs. Despite that, CG music is pretty nice as a whole!
  3. Anime: I still haven't watched it yet, but... Due to the nature of the series, it only focuses on 14 idols. If any of these 14 girls interest you, I would reccomend watching it here on Crunchyroll.
  4. Story Translations: CG translations are scattered all over the internet. It's mostly Deresute and not Mobamas, but you can find a good amount and variety here on Idolmaster Subs. Also, there are a lot of official comic translations on Danbooru which give you a quicker look into the characters' lives.
  5. Manga: TBA.
  6. Mobamas: I like Mobamas, but I probably wouldn't reccomend playing it until you have an idol you like enough to save up for. It's much more competitive than Deresute, although there is the bonus that you can trade your cards with other players if you have a verified Mobage account. I don't think there's an English guide to playing Mobamas, but you can find how to start a Mobage account on the SideM Wiki.
Million Live!
Million Live! is the second mobile spinoff. It has a smaller cast of characters that work at 765Pro, including the 765AS idols who now act more as mentors. ML currently has one active game, Theater Days (Mirishita). It previously had a browser game similar to Mobamas called Million Live! (Greemas).
  1. Mirishita: Mirishita is pretty much the game as Deresute but with a slightly different setup and art style. I think which one you want to play depends on what music you prefer as well as the characters. Since ML has a smaller cast than CG, you might prefer it if you want to get specific idols. You can download the game here.
  2. Songs: ML has a younger cast, so their voices tend to be a bit high-pitched compared to the rest of iM@S. But if that doesn't bother you, I would totally reccomend listening to ML music as I think it has some of the best music in all of iM@S!
  3. Story Translations: ML translations are quite a bit easier to find than CG (still, it's mostly Mirishita and no Greemas). Once again, you can look here on Idolmaster Subs and here on Sankyuu ML Subs.
  4. Movie: A couple of ML characters appeared in the iM@S 765AS movie in 2015. I'd say that if you're watching the 765AS anime, you should watch the movie anyway (plus, it's important for SideM!)
SideM
SideM is the third mobile spinoff. It's notable for being the only series to focus on male idols as well has having idols that formerly appeared as rivals in the console game iDOLM@STER 2 (another idol appeared as a crossdressing playable character in a Nintendo DS spinoff in 2009). SideM currently has one active game, SideM (SideMoba). It previously had a rhythm game called LIVE ON STAGE! (LOS/Emusute). A new rhythm game will release sometime in the future called GROWING STARS! (Saisuta).
  1. Story Translations: In particular, I'm reccomending the character magazines. Each character has a tooon of magazines, which are little comics that feature them. And a lot of them are translated! Also, all of SideM's translated content is in one centralized area, so you don't have to search around the whole internet for them! Nearly half of all SideM content has been translated, so it's easy to find stories about the characters you love. Please take a look!
  2. Songs: Unlike the other iM@S branches so far, SideM is split into units of 2~5 members, each with a consistent theme. The music has a wider variety of genres as well as deeper voices, so if you prefer music that's a bit more mature sounding (or you just like men), SideM is your best bet.
  3. Anime: The SideM anime is a cute and short anime focusing on around half of the idols. I still think it's enjoyable even if you're not particularily interested in them, but I would reccomend watching the iM@S 2011 anime and movie first. It's here on Crunchyroll.
  4. SideMoba: I play SideMoba daily, but like Mobamas, it's very difficult to get into for first time players. However, I would still reccomend playing it if you have an idol you care about enough to rank for, since you can also trade for cards you like. Also compared to Mobamas, there's a lot of detailed guides on how to play for English speakers. You can find the beginner's guide here on the SideM Wiki and more detailed guides SideM_Guides.
Shiny Colors
Shiny Colors is the fourth mobile spinoff. It doesn't have a ryhthm or social game, but rather, a "produce" game with heavy focus on story. Like SideM, the idols are split into units of 2~5 members.
  1. Shiny Colors: The game is very accesible to new players, being playable in the browser and having a constantly updating English Patch. It's honestly a very fun and unqiue game that's unlike anything else I've tried; plus, the artwork is gorgeous and it's very generous compared to other iM@S games. You can play it here.
  2. Story Translations: SC absolutely has the best story and writing of every iM@S mobile game. The only reason the translations are ranked lower than the game itself is that it's probably a better experience to read the stories through the English patch. If you want to see the stories on their own, though, they can be found here on Maebatsu's YouTube channel, and additional translations are here on Idolmaster Subs.
  3. Songs: TBA.
OTHER
There are a few other iM@S spinoffs that aren't considered to be part of the main five.

Dearly Stars was released in 2009 for the Nintendo DS as a spinoff for the main iM@S series. It's the first appearance of Ryo Akizuki, who later appears in SideM as a male idol (in an alternate timeline, the characters also appeared in Cinerella Girls). There are a few game scene translations by habatakisoul. The DS manga are also fully translated.

THE iDOLM@STER.KR was a Korean drama that aired in 2017 following a group of K-Pop idols. It's quite different from regular iM@S in that it's fully live action and features a more serious, dramatic plot line. However, it does cover some original iM@S songs in Korean. You can watch it here on Amazon Prime.

POP LINKS! is a mobile spinoff featuring all five of the main branches. It's a puzzle game where your goal is to pop as many note bubbles as possible in a short amount of time. You can collect all of the different iDOLM@STER characters, although not every idol has been added to the game yet. The game is easy to learn and understand, plus you can meet idols from the other branches that you might not know yet. It's downloadable here.
